Project Context

This project analyzes a Purdue University dataset of 1,534 major U.S. power outages (2000–2016) to predict outage duration using only information available at the moment an outage begins (e.g., cause, region, state, urban density, day-of-week). Accurately forecasting duration helps utilities allocate repair crews, pre-stage resources, and set restoration expectations.

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Using pandas and NumPy, we cleaned the raw data—dropped metadata rows, removed 58 events missing OUTAGE.DURATION, and discarded irrelevant land-use and macroeconomic columns. We combined separate date and time fields into OUTAGE.START and OUTAGE.RESTORATION timestamps, from which we engineered dow (day of week) and an is_weekend flag, and retained key severity metrics (CUSTOMERS.AFFECTED, DEMAND.LOSS.MW) along with state urban density (POPDEN_URBAN). We visualized outage frequencies by cause and state (bar charts, U.S. choropleth) and examined duration distributions across climate regions and seasons (boxplots) and urbanization vs. customers affected (scatterplot) using Matplotlib and Plotly. Hypothesis tests in SciPy (permutation-based missingness test, Mann–Whitney U) confirmed that missingness in DEMAND.LOSS.MW depended on cause (p < 0.001) and that seasonality did not significantly affect duration.

Data Modeling

In scikit-learn, we first built a baseline Pipeline: a ColumnTransformer with OneHotEncoder(handle_unknown="ignore") on CAUSE.CATEGORY and CLIMATE.REGION, feeding a RandomForestRegressor(n_estimators=100). This yielded Test RMSE ≈ 7 398 min (≈ 5.1 days) and R² = 0.108, showing limited predictive power from cause and region alone. For the final model, we added predictors (U.S._STATE, POPDEN_URBAN) and engineered features (dow, is_weekend), standard-scaled the numeric feature, and replaced the forest with a HistGradientBoostingRegressor. We performed a GridSearchCV (5-fold, optimizing RMSE) over learning_rate (0.01, 0.05, 0.1), max_iter (200, 400, 600), and max_leaf_nodes (15, 31, 63), selecting learning_rate=0.01, max_iter=200, max_leaf_nodes=15.

Results & Fairness Analysis

The tuned Gradient-Boosting model reduced test RMSE to 1 329.56 minutes (≈ 22 hours) with R² = 0.3353, a fourfold improvement in error. Finally, we conducted a custom permutation test to compare RMSE for outages in the “South” vs. “East North Central” regions; with p = 0.459, we found no evidence of disparate predictive performance across these climate regions.
